Supplierscave - User Guide

Process Flow chart

Modules:

  1. Buyer Module
    1. Enquiry

      2. Buyers can create enquiries, by selecting the project name and uploading the details of the enquiry.

      The enquiry can then be sent to multiple Suppliers by selecting their email addresses. Personalised emails are sent to Suppliers.

      The details of the offer can be updated by the Buyer from time to time. The Manager can access the status and reports anytime in their dashboards.

    2. Purchase Requisition

      3. The Purchase requisition is a request to buy Items for the Project. The User need to select the Project, Customer and upload the details of material to be purchased using the CSV file format.

    3. Purchase order

      A Purchase order is issued with an intent to buy from a Supplier. The Purchase requisition is a request to buy Items for the Project. The User need to select the Project, Customer and upload the details of material to be purchased using the CSV file format.

      1. Prime Data

        2. The Prime Data includes the details of the Buyer, Supplier, Contact persons, Summary of Project and Items of Purchase along with the value and terms of purchase.

      2. Annexure – 1

        3. Annexure – 1 contains the details of the Items along with Price, Taxes duties, Delivery Terms and Payment Terms. The items can be selected from PR and the relevant details for a Purchase Order such as Price, Taxes, Delivery and terms can be updated.

      3. Annexure – 2

        4. Annexure – 2 contains the detailed Terms & Conditions of the Purchase along with Payment Terms, Liquidated damages. The data from payment terms automatically generates the Projected Cash-outflows.

    4. Goods Receipt Note (GRN)

      5. Goods Receipt Note (GRN) are created when the Goods are received at the Yard or warehouse as per the Purchase order and Shipping Documents. The Store in-charge verifies the documents and the physical goods received.

    5. Inward Material Inspection Note (IMIN)

      6. Inward Material Inspection Note (IMIN) are created after inspection of the Goods received at the Yard or warehouse as per the Purchase order, Shipping Documents, Engineering and, inspection and Test Reports. The Quality inspector verifies the documents and the physical goods received and records his observation.

    6. Payment Requests

      7. Payment Requests are created based on the invoice received and the completion of work certified by the authorised person. .

    7. Cash-outflow reports
      1. Projected Payments (Cash Out-Flows)

        2. The projected cash outflows are captured based on the inputs in Purchase order which are: (a) Currency & Value of items in Purchase Order, (b) milestone values, (c) expected mile stone date and (d) credit period.

      2. Payments Due (Cash Out-Flows)

        3. The payments due (cash outflows) are captured once a Payment Request is created.

      3. Payments Made

        4. The payments due (cash outflows) are captured once a Payment Request status is changed from created to Paid.

    8. Supplier Management

      9. The database of Suppliers is saved here. The Supplier Database created is accessible to all Buyers and across all Modules.

  2. Stores
    1. Inventory:

      2. Once a GRN is made for a PO, the Material details and the quantity are reflected in the inventory. The material is received by default in default inward store.

    2. Material Requisition

      3. Material requisition is a Request to transfer material, issue material for production, sale and despatch to a Sub-vendor’s place for further processing. They are classified as Material issue and Delivery notes.

      1. Material issue:

        Material requests for material movement within the organisation are referred to as Material issue. They are further classified as:

        1. Material Requisition (Transfers)
        2. Material Requisition (Processing)
      2. Delivery Notes:

        Material requests for material movement outside the organisation are referred to as Delivery Notes. They are further classified as:

        1. Delivery Notes (Sale):

          2. Delivery notes created to supply material to Customer against a sales order and invoiced to receive a payment against Supply.

        2. Delivery Notes (Non-Billable)

          Delivery notes created to supply material to Customer / Sub-vendor that are not invoiced.

          1. Delivery Notes (Non-Billable, Returnable)

            Delivery notes created to supply material to Customer/ Sub-vendor not for the purpose of sale, with an expectation to receive the material back after the intended use.

          2. Delivery Notes (Non-Billable, Non- Returnable)

            Delivery notes created to supply material to Customer/ Sub-vendor not for the purpose of sale, with no expectations to receive the material back. These could be accessories which are billed as part of the main order that are sent separately and hence not required to be billed.
